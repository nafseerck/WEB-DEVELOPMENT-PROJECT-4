MACHINAMENTUM

MACHINAMENTUM is a Tower Crane Design event organized by Civil Engineering Department as a part of the South Indian Techno-Cultural fest QUASSO LIBERUM 5 (2k16) in which the participants are required to build a tower crane using pop sticks which supports two counter balancing loads at different ends of cantilever in accordance with design constraints. The main objective is to design a tower crane that can sustain extensive moments and loads, thus taking the concept of planar structures to the next level. Lift your ideas using a Tower Crane…!!

SPECIFICATION

• Make a tower crane, the structure must be made only from Popsicle sticks and adhesive, which consists of a vertical mast and a lever arm to support loads.
• The crane (point of loading) must be able to reach a height of 60cm and have a range of 60cm; an error of 10% is allowable.
• The main mast starting from base should be vertical.
• The base and vertical mast should be within an area of 20x20 cm at any cross-section.
• The lever-arm should have an area less than 20x20 cm at any cross-section.
• The base must have extensions of 3- 4 cm at the base on both sides along the lever-arm direction, to hold the structure to the ground.
• Hooks must be provided at the ends of the lever arm to support the load where the structure would be loaded and counter- loaded.
• For counter balancing load, loading pan and weights will be provided at the competition. Participants will be allowed to increase counter-balance load on the loading pan only three times after the commencement of loading.
• Hook on the Counter-balancing side must be at least 20cm apart from the main structure.
• The structure should be a truss & not a beam.
• Overlap criteria - Not more than 2 sticks must have a complete side to side overlap
• Joint Criteria - Overlap of two members at a joint must not be greater than 20mm on either side
• No lateral supports in the form of struts should be present.
• The Popsicle sticks may be cut or trimmed to any shape or size.
• The adhesive can only be used to join Popsicle sticks together; however adhesives cannot be applied on the free surface of the member sticks to increase its strength.
• Failing to abide by above terms will lead to immediate disqualification

MATERIALS PROVIDED

• Pop sticks - Maximum of 800 per team.
• Minimum adhesive is provided (No extra will be provided).
